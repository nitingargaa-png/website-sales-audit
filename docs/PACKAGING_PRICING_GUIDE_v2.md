## THE CORE PRINCIPLE

You are not selling websites. You are selling a **lead generation and follow-up system**
that happens to include a website. This reframe matters because:

- A website alone: $500–$2,000 (commodity, easy to price-shop)
- A lead generation system: $1,500–$5,000+ setup (hard to compare, clear ROI)

Every package you sell should answer the business owner's real question:
**"Will this get me more customers?"** — not "will I have a nicer website."

## WHICH PACKAGE TO PITCH — DRIVEN BY THE AUDIT SCORE

Every prospect gets a weighted health score out of 100 from the audit
(see docs/SKILL.md v12 Phase 2 — WEIGHTED HEALTH SCORE). The score drives
which package you lead with.

| Audit Score / Band | Pitch Tier | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **0–40** (🔴 Needs Significant Work) | Package 2 or Package 3 minimum | Site is actively costing them customers — a Foundation-only fix leaves too many gaps. Rebuild + automation is the right answer. |
| **41–65** (🟡 Some Gaps) | Package 1 + automation add-ons OR Package 2 | Site is functional but leaky. Use the two-option close (see THE CLOSE in SALES_SCRIPT_v2.md). |
| **66–100** (🟢 Strong Foundation) | Automation-only — no rebuild | Site is doing its job. Pitch the missing automation layer only (missed-call text-back, review automation, Voice AI, etc.). |

## TYPE A vs TYPE B PROSPECTS — OVERRIDES THE SCORE RULE

| Type | Signals | Pitch |
|---|---|---|
| **Type A — Small/Independent** | 1–5 trucks, Wix/Squarespace/WordPress DIY, Gmail email, under 50 reviews, no FSM tool, phone number only for contact | Pitch the full system (rebuild + automation). Owner is the decision-maker. Make it personal, fast close. |
| **Type B — Established/Enterprise** | 6+ trucks, ServiceTitan or Housecall Pro booking, 100+ reviews, financing widget, membership/maintenance plan, multi-location | **DO NOT pitch a website rebuild.** DO NOT pitch basic booking. Pitch the automation layer their existing systems don't provide: post-job follow-up, review velocity, missed-call text-back, Voice AI overflow. Decision-maker is the GM or Ops Manager — NOT the owner. Longer sales cycle. ROI framing is critical. |

A Type B prospect with a 35/100 score still does NOT get a rebuild pitch —
their score likely reflects a Scorpion or enterprise-built site that is "solid
enough," and the real gaps are in the automation layer their FSM can't cover.

## THREE PACKAGES

---

### PACKAGE 1 — FOUNDATION
**"Your business, online and looking professional"**

Best for: Businesses with a very basic or broken site, low budget, or skeptical first-time buyers.
Goal: Get them off a free builder, establish credibility, prove your value.

**What's included:**
- New website (up to 5 pages: Home, Services, About, Contact, Thank You)
- Mobile-friendly, fast-loading, HTTPS secure
- Professional domain email setup (name@businessdomain.com)
- Tap-to-call phone number on every page
- Quote request form (connected to their email + basic auto-reply)
- Google Business Profile setup or optimization
- Local search setup (city + trade in page titles and descriptions so Google
  can match the site to nearby searches) + LocalBusiness structured data
- Basic lead follow-up: form submission triggers an auto-reply email to the prospect
  *(Automated text follow-up and full CRM pipeline are Package 2 upgrades)*

**What's NOT included:**
- GHL full automation suite
- CRM pipeline with stage tracking
- Missed call text-back
- Appointment booking calendar
- Review request automation
- Monthly management

**Pricing:**
- Setup fee: **$997 – $1,497**
- Monthly: **$97/month** (hosting, minor updates, uptime monitoring)
  OR one-time handoff with no retainer

**Upsell path:** After 30 days live, send this email:
*"Hey [Name], your new site has been live a month — I pulled a quick look at how
it's performing and wanted to share something. One thing I notice with [trade]
businesses at this stage is that calls are coming in but there's no automatic
follow-up when a job is missed or a form sits in an inbox. Do you have 15 minutes
this week to look at what that would cost you to fix?"*

This opens the upgrade conversation to Package 2 without pressure. Most Package 1
clients who are happy with the site will convert within 60–90 days.

### PACKAGE 2 — GROWTH SYSTEM
**"Your website works — now let's make sure every lead gets followed up"**

Best for: Businesses ready to invest, currently missing leads, or already have some web presence.
Goal: Build the full lead capture + follow-up system. This is your sweet spot.

**What's included — everything in Package 1, plus the TIER 1 CORE
GHL automations from SKILL.md v12:**
- GHL subaccount setup
- Lead follow-up workflow (auto text + email within 5 minutes of form submission)
- Missed call text-back (auto SMS within 60 seconds of any unanswered call)
- Online booking calendar (embedded on site, auto-confirmation on booking)
- SMS chat widget on website (after-hours capture, routes to CRM)
- CRM pipeline setup (New Lead → Contacted → Booked → Completed → Won)
- Client login to GHL to see their leads
- 1-hour onboarding call to walk them through the system

**Pricing:**
- Setup fee: **$2,497 – $3,497**
- Monthly retainer: **$297/month**
  Covers: GHL platform cost (see COGS section below), monthly maintenance,
  pipeline monitoring, GBP review management, minor site updates, monthly performance summary

**Why the monthly makes sense to the client:**
"The monthly covers your GHL account, keeps everything running, and I check in
every month to make sure leads aren't falling through the cracks. Most clients
find it's like having a part-time marketing person for less than $300/month."

### PACKAGE 3 — FULL SYSTEM
**"Everything working together — website, automation, reviews, and visibility"**

Best for: Established businesses, higher revenue trades (HVAC, roofing, electrical),
or businesses actively losing to competitors online.
Goal: Become the most visible, most trusted, most responsive business in their area.

**What's included — everything in Package 2, plus the TIER 2 GROWTH and
select TIER 3 SCALE automations from SKILL.md v12:**
- Automated review request system (post-job SMS/email → Google review link) — TIER 2
- Appointment reminder sequence (24-hr and 2-hr pre-appointment SMS) — TIER 2
- Estimate / invoice follow-up automation — TIER 2
- Unified inbox (SMS, email, Facebook DMs, Instagram DMs in one place) — TIER 2
- Voice AI receptionist (overflow + after-hours — handles inbound calls,
  captures job type and address, books appointments or routes to dispatch) — TIER 3
  *Separate from the SMS chat widget in Package 2. Requires GHL AI Employee
  add-on (~$97/month per sub-account) plus per-minute usage — see note below.*
- Monthly Google review response management
- Monthly GBP post (1 post/month keeping the listing active)
- Quarterly website refresh (update photos, promotions, seasonal content)
- Monthly report with: new leads, bookings, reviews received, GBP views

**Pricing:**
- Setup fee: **$3,997 – $5,497**
- Monthly retainer: **$497/month base** (excludes Voice AI variable usage — see below)

**Voice AI cost note (mandatory disclosure before quoting):**
The $497/month covers the platform license and TIER 2 automations.
Voice AI is a separate GHL AI Employee add-on at ~$97/month per sub-account,
PLUS per-minute telephony charges billed by GHL at ~$0.05–$0.15/minute
(voice engine + LLM tokens). A busy trades business at 500 inbound minutes/month
adds ~$50–$75 in usage costs to your agency bill on top of the AI Employee license.

Quoting options — pick one and document it in the client agreement:
- **Pass-through model:** Client pays $497/month + actual usage. Show them the
  GHL usage dashboard. Most transparent, no margin risk.
- **Flat-rate with cap:** "$497/month covers up to 300 voice minutes. Heavy usage
  billed at cost." Protects you on low-volume accounts; fair on high-volume.
- **Blended rate:** Price at $547–$597/month and absorb typical usage. Simpler
  for the client; works if average account stays under 400 min/month.

**The pitch:**
"This is the full picture. Your website brings them in, your automated follow-up
catches every lead, your review system builds your reputation automatically,
and every month you get a report showing exactly what it's doing for your business.
Most of my clients in [trade] recoup this in the first 1–2 new customers a month."

## HOW TO PRICE FOR YOUR MARKET

These are starting points — adjust based on:

**Charge more if:**
- The trade has high job values (HVAC, roofing, electrical — jobs worth $2K–$20K)
- They're in a competitive market (GTA, Vancouver, Calgary)
- They're currently losing obvious leads (broken site, no GBP, zero reviews)
- They've expressed budget comfort or are clearly established

**Founding client pricing (first 3–5 clients only):**
Rather than discounting, offer a **"Case Study Credit":**
> "The setup fee is $2,497. For my first few clients in this area, I offer a $500
> credit in exchange for a video testimonial and permission to show your results
> to other [trade] businesses. The monthly stays the same. This way I have proof
> to show future clients, and you get a better deal for being early."

This approach:
- Maintains your price integrity (you're not responding to objection with a discount)
- Gives you a rationale that positions scarcity rather than desperation
- Gets you the testimonial you need to close future deals at full price

**The "never discount" rule — clarified:**
The rule applies to **reactive discounting** (dropping your price because someone
pushed back). Founding client pricing is a proactive market-entry strategy — it's
deliberate and time-limited, not a response to "can you do it cheaper?"

Once you have 3 case studies with real results, move to full price exclusively.
At that point, if someone asks for a discount: "My pricing is what it is — I have
clients getting [specific result] at this price point, and I can show you exactly
what that looks like for your business."

## WHAT YOUR MONTHLY RETAINER ACTUALLY COSTS YOU

Know your margins before you quote — and understand how they shift with client count.

**GHL platform cost model:**

GHL Agency Pro (SaaS Mode) is a **flat $497/month** for unlimited subaccounts.
Your per-client platform cost is not fixed — it's $497 divided by your active clients:

| Active Clients | GHL Cost/Client | + SMS Usage/Client | Total Platform COGS/Client |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3 clients | ~$166 | ~$10–$15 | ~$176–$181 |
| 5 clients | ~$99 | ~$10–$15 | ~$109–$114 |
| 10 clients | ~$50 | ~$10–$15 | ~$60–$65 |
| 20 clients | ~$25 | ~$10–$15 | ~$35–$40 |

**Additional COGS to account for:**

- **Netlify hosting:** $0 free tier (up to 4 clients). $19/month Pro at 5+ clients.
  At 10 clients: adds ~$2/client/month. Negligible but document it.
  ⚠️ Free tier suspension risk: exceeding limits pauses **all** client sites until
  next billing cycle — not just the offending one. Upgrade trigger: 5 clients OR
  any single site approaching 50GB/month, whichever comes first.

- **Voice AI (Package 3 only):** $97/month AI Employee license + ~$0.05–$0.15/min
  telephony usage. At 500 min/month: adds $50–$75 in variable costs per Package 3
  client. Either pass through to client or price Package 3 retainer at $547–$597
  to absorb typical usage. Never quote Package 3 as a flat rate without disclosing
  the variable telephony component.

**SMS usage note:** LC Phone SMS costs ~$0.008–$0.01 per SMS segment. A trades
client with active lead follow-up, missed call texts, and review requests may
send 150–300 SMS/month (~$1.50–$3/month per client). Estimate $10–$15/month
to be safe — or better, **enable SaaS Mode re-billing so clients pay their own
SMS/email usage directly.** This protects your $297/month margin completely.

> **Critical action item:** Enable GHL SaaS Mode re-billing before your first client
> goes live. Without it, if a client has a high-volume month (e.g., 500+ texts from
> a promotion), that usage hits your agency account bill.

**Your margin at different scale:**

| Active Clients | Your Revenue | GHL Platform | Your Net (before time) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3 clients at $297/mo | $891 | $497 | $394/mo |
| 10 clients at $297/mo | $2,970 | $497 | $2,473/mo |
| 20 clients at $297/mo | $5,940 | $497 | $5,443/mo |

This is why scale matters — the fixed GHL cost becomes negligible as you grow.
The break-even point where your retainer income meaningfully exceeds overhead
is approximately 7–8 clients.

## THE MATH TO $10K/MONTH — REALISTIC RAMP

**Steady-state target (what the numbers look like when running):**
- 3 Package 2 setups/month ($2,497 each) = $7,491 + retainers building
- OR: 2 Package 3 setups/month ($3,997 each) = $7,994 + retainers building
- 20 retainer clients at $297/month = $5,940/month recurring (baseline income)

**Three-phase ramp (realistic for a solo operator):**

**Months 1–3 — Proof of concept**
- Goal: 1 setup/month, build 3–5 retainer clients
- Revenue: ~$2,500–$5,000 setup income + ~$900–$1,500 retainer income
- Focus: Nail delivery, get your first testimonial, refine the script

**Months 4–6 — Building momentum**
- Goal: 2 setups/month, grow retainer base to 8–10 clients
- Revenue: ~$5,000 setup income + ~$2,400–$3,000 retainer income
- Focus: Systematize delivery, build referral relationships, start with GBP posts

**Month 7+ — Scaling toward $10K**
- Goal: 3 setups/month, retainer base growing to 15–20 clients
- Revenue: $7,500+ setup + $4,500–$6,000 retainer = $10K+ in range
- Focus: Delivery speed, word-of-mouth, upsells to existing clients

**Year 1 conservative reality check:**
Most solo operators with a full-time job land 8–12 retainer clients in Year 1
($2,400–$3,600/month recurring) plus sporadic setup income. That's $4,000–$6,000/month
by Month 12 — meaningful progress, not $10K yet. $10K is a Month 12–18 milestone
for most people who treat this seriously. Plan accordingly so you're not discouraged.

## HOW TO PRESENT PACKAGES IN THE SALES CONVERSATION

Never lead with the package names or prices. Lead with what you found in the audit.

**Formula:**
1. Show them the problem (use the audit report)
2. Show them the cost of the problem ("a plumber typically earns $300–$800 per call...")
3. Show the ROI ("two extra jobs a month at $500 each = $1,000 — more than covers the retainer")
4. Show them the solution (the system, not the website)
5. Present two options (never one — anchoring works better with a choice)

**ROI anchors by trade:**
- **Plumbing:** Average emergency call $350–$600. Two extra calls/month = $700–$1,200.
- **HVAC:** Average install $3,000–$8,000. One extra job/month = 10x retainer cost.
- **Roofing:** Average job $5,000–$15,000. One extra job/quarter = $20K+ annual uplift.
- **Electrical:** Average job $400–$2,000. Three extra calls/month = retainer paid.
- **Cleaning:** Average recurring client $200–$400/month. Three extra clients = $600–$1,200/mo.
  *(Note: cleaning is a natural expansion from the core trades verticals — use this anchor
  if you're targeting residential cleaning businesses, but confirm your market before
  including it in your standard pitch. Cleaning has different seasonality and job values
  than plumbing/HVAC.)*

**Example close:**
"Based on what I found, I'd recommend starting with the Growth System.
It gets you off that free builder, sets up automatic follow-up so you never
miss a lead again, and includes the booking calendar so customers can schedule
directly from your site. Setup is $2,497 and then $297/month for me to keep
everything running and send you a monthly report. Or if you want to start smaller
and just get the website right first, the Foundation package is $997 to start.
Which direction feels right for where you are right now?"

## PAYMENT TERMS (use these consistently — applies to all packages)

**Standard terms:**
- **Setup fee:** 50% due before work begins, 50% due on go-live
- **Monthly retainer:** Charged on the 1st of each month, auto-pay preferred
- **Late payment:** Net 7 days. After 7 days, site maintenance pauses until paid.
  Do not threaten this — just have it in writing so there's no ambiguity.
- **Cancellation:** 30-day written notice required to cancel retainer.
  Setup fee is non-refundable once work has begun.

**How to collect:**
- Setup deposit: GHL payment link or e-transfer (Canadian clients)
- Ongoing retainer: GHL recurring invoice (auto-charges saved card)
- Never start production work without the deposit in hand

**If a prospect pushes back on the split:**
"The 50% upfront covers my time setting up your GHL account, building the site,
and configuring all the automations before launch. The second half is due when
you see it live — you're not paying for the whole thing until you can see it."

**Discount policy:**
Never discount the retainer. Discounting the setup fee for a founding client is
acceptable only when framed as a "Case Study Credit" — documented in the agreement
as a reduced rate in exchange for a testimonial and permission to use results as
a case study. This is not a discount; it's a trade.
